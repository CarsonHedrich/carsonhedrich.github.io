Selected work

01 / MALWARE RE

Harulf Virus - Reverse Engineering Analysis

Performed static and dynamic analysis on a packed, polymorphic Windows PE virus to determine its infection mechanism and payload behavior. Applied OllyDbg sandboxing to safely unpack the virus and extract the unencrypted binary, then conducted assembly-level analysis in IDA Pro. Identified and documented the virus's polymorphic evasion capabilities - findings applicable to improving antivirus threat definitions and EDR evasion research.

IDA Pro OllyDbg Ghidra Static Analysis Dynamic Analysis x86 Assembly

02 / DIGITAL FORENSICS

Cybercrime Investigation - Simulated Case

Led a full forensic investigation of a simulated suspect scenario. Recovered evidence from steganography-concealed messages, obfuscated files, suspicious browser history, and network packet traces using Autopsy. Exploited vulnerabilities in suspect-controlled websites to extract additional evidence. Produced a complete chain-of-custody report documenting findings and establishing proof of illegal activity.

Multi-Sample Malware Analysis Suite

Analyzed a series of malware samples spanning MS-DOS to 32-bit Windows: Michelangelo (boot sector virus), Dos7, SQLSlammer (network worm), Lucius, and Harulf. Used static and dynamic methods to compare infection techniques, payload delivery, and evasion strategies across generations of malware.
