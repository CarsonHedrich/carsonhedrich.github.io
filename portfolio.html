Learning Outcomes

Rhetoric

Major Project 3 provides an excellent example of applying rhetoric in this course. In MP3, we created 3 deliverables/documents, each with a different purpose, audience, and context for its use. This required deliberate consideration of how to properly tailor each to the intended audience. I’d like to examine the rhetorical choices for the brochure template we created to showcase this learning outcome.

For the brochure template, our audience was prospective members that may be interested in the club. As the lead on the brochure in my group, I made the important decisions regarding tone, visuals, and what information to include. Because the purpose of the brochure is to attract new students to join the club, a major rhetorical choice I made was to use friendly, informal language, almost as if the brochure was speaking directly to the reader. The previous game visuals in the brochure are intended to grab the audience’s attention, while the last part of the brochure has the call to actions to check out VGDev’s website and to join their Discord.

For additional rationale behind the other deliverables, my MP3 Collaboration Reflection Memo has more in-depth information in the “Communication Choices” section.

Process

The idea of assignments as an iterative process has been present for me throughout this course. I started most deliverables (including this self-assessment) with just short, detail-oriented bullet points as a brainstorming process to get my ideas written down before moving on to filling in the rest. In MP2, I did this with my partner, David, to frame the most important parts of our research about the CrowdStrike incident. We also used the document to gather research sources and write down questions to consider while we conducted our research. I’ll link the document here.

This document represents an important part of the process for Major Project 2. We began our information collecting in this document, and starting considering what would be most important to our audience (the class and our instructor) to help us decide what should later be included on our poster.

MP2 also represents the iterative and revision-based nature of technical communication, seen best in the differences between our draft and final poster. In our draft, we have almost too much whitespace, our graph is not directly relevant to our research question, and we were lacking some of the analysis in our discussion. We realized these shortcomings through feedback from our peers and instructor, as well as self-reflection, and were able to revise these things to improve them for our final poster. In contrast with the issues I described, our final graphic is important context for answering our research question, and we were able to revise our results and discussion sections to focus on the more important pieces of our findings.

Modes and Media

The multimedia nature of our MP3 deliverables provides another opportunity to examine them in relation to a learning outcome. Each of our deliverables utilizes multiple WOVEN components. Our brochure template, utilizes friendly, persuasive writing, visuals of previous games’ titles, and a colorful scheme that is meant to catch passerby’s eyes. Our Github demo video on the other hand utilizes mostly oral and visual components. The oral component provides the majority of the tutorial information, while the visuals on screen keep watchers interested, and supports their learning by providing a 1-to-1 of what their screens should look like as an example.

Each individual WOVEN component of our deliverables would be useful in their own way, but we were able to effectively combine them to create more compelling deliverables that serve their purpose all the more effectively.

Design

To illustrate how I applied design this semester, I’d like to showcase my resume from MP1. I made several considerations on how to most effectively design and organize my resume, considering importance of information, how to provide information concisely, and how to maintain high clarity. When choosing and ordering the sections, I thought about what a recruiter might first want to see from a new college graduate, and used this to outline my resume. Further design decisions I made (along with using feedback from others) were to bold the buzzwords relating to cybersecurity skills and to left-align section and subsection headers, where the recruiter’s eyes would likely be trailing down. Overall, these decisions helped to make my resume accessible and concise, but also eye-catching in the areas that are most important.

Major Project 3 - VGDev Technical Communication Toolkit

In this project, we created a technical communication toolkit for the Georgia Tech student organization VGDev. We first looked at the technical communication needs of the organization, and found that their on-campus recruiting as well as new member onboarding could be improved. To this end, we created two different deliverables. The first was a brochure template to recruit new members, while the second was a Github demo video to teach freshman and other new members new to Github the VGDev workflow using Github. Finally, we created a recommendation report to be presented to VGDev leadership to persuade them to use the materials we created, and provide any other useful recommendations we came up with. This project required a lot of thought put into the rhetoric behind the deliverables to most effectively persuade our audience for the deliverable's intended purpose. We considered who might read/use our materials, and how best to communicate with them.
